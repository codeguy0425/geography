4.2 What are the characteristics of urban decay in old urban areas?

Section C favourite — expect a photo or map extract of an old district (Sham Shui Po, Yau Ma Tei, Wan Chai) with labelled questions.

4.2.1 Land use conflicts

Land use conflict occurs when two incompatible land uses are too close to each other.

  • Residential vs. markets and garages — residential buildings located near markets and garages create a noisy and unpleasant living environment
  • Residential vs. dangerous facilities — some people live close to hazardous facilities (e.g. gas stations, factories) that threaten their safety
  • Mixed industrial and residential uses — factories next to flats (e.g. old Tsuen Wan, Cheung Sha Wan) cause noise, odour, and fire hazards
  • Hawker zones vs. residents — street markets bring noise and rubbish alongside livelihood; tension between stall holders and nearby residents

4.2.2 Old and worn-out buildings

Many buildings in old urban areas are over 40 years old and lack proper repair and maintenance.

  • Dilapidated appearance — cracked walls, peeling paint, rusted railings, exposed rebar
  • Subdivided flats (勧房) — single flat divided into 2+ smaller units with shared kitchen/toilet; multiple electricity meters on one landing
  • Cage homes — partitioned bed spaces enclosed by metal mesh, ~0.5–1 m² per person
  • Tong lau (唐楼) — pre-1960s tenement buildings, 3–6 storeys, no lifts, shared bathrooms; common in Wan Chai, Sheung Wan, Sham Shui Po
  • Lack of maintenance — many buildings have no owners' corporation to organise repairs; owners often cannot agree or afford the cost
  • Risk of collapse — buildings with poor structure may collapse, endangering lives
  • Other safety hazards — illegal rooftop structures, exposed wiring, blocked fire exits, no sprinklers
  • Strata-title ownership — each flat owner holds a share; redevelopment requires 80–90% owner consent, so repairs are often neglected

4.2.3 Lack of facilities

In old urban areas, there is a general lack of open space such as parks and green belts.

  • No lifts in old tong lau — inaccessible for elderly and disabled residents
  • Lack of open space — insufficient parks, green belts, and recreational areas
  • Insufficient community and recreational facilities — existing facilities are inadequate or in poor condition
  • Poor sanitation — shared toilets, inadequate sewage systems, rubbish accumulation in back alleys
  • Inadequate parking and narrow streets — no off-street parking; loading/unloading blocks traffic
  • Poor ventilation and natural lighting — buildings built close together; narrow alleys restrict airflow and sunlight
