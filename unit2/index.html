Unit 2 – Is Tourism Good or Bad for Local People?

E7 Tourism · S1 Geography Final Revision
Appears in Section C
📌 Exam tip: Unit 2 appears in Section C (Structured Questions). You will be asked to describe benefits and/or problems of tourism in a host area. Always give specific examples and explain both economic and socio-cultural impacts for higher marks.

Units in this topic

2.1 Benefits of Tourism

Economic, social, environmental, and cultural benefits tourists bring to host areas

2.2 Problems of Tourism

Economic, environmental, social, and cultural problems caused by tourism

Key terms — quick revision cards

Host area
The place that tourists visit and where tourism takes place
Economic benefit
Positive financial impact such as jobs, income, and tax revenue
Social benefit
Improved services and living standards for local people
Environmental conservation
Protecting nature and wildlife through tourism awareness
Cultural exchange
Sharing of traditions and ideas between tourists and locals
Cultural heritage
Historic buildings, traditions, and customs preserved for future generations
Over-reliance on tourism
Depending too heavily on tourism, risking economic instability
Overcrowding
Too many tourists in one area, causing discomfort for locals
Cultural conflict
Tension between tourists' behaviour and local customs
Loss of local characteristics
Traditional culture fading due to tourist influence

Exam-style practice questions

Multiple Choice (1 mark each)

Q1
Which of the following is an economic benefit of tourism?
A. Cultural exchange
B. Providing jobs and income to local people
C. Environmental pollution
D. Loss of local characteristics
Q2
Which of the following is a social problem caused by tourism?
A. Higher tax revenue
B. Preservation of cultural heritage
C. Overcrowding and noise disturbing local people
D. Improved infrastructure
Q3
Over-reliance on tourism is dangerous because:
A. It increases cultural exchange
B. A drop in tourist numbers can badly affect the economy
C. It protects the environment
D. It creates more job opportunities

True or False (1 mark each)

Q4
"Tourism only brings economic benefits to host areas."
False — tourism also brings social, environmental, cultural benefits as well as problems.
Q5
"Tourism can help preserve cultural heritage."
True — tourism creates incentives to preserve historic buildings, traditions, and customs.

Structured Question (6 marks)

Q6

(a) Describe two economic benefits of tourism for a host area. (2 marks)

(b) Explain two social or environmental problems caused by tourism. (4 marks)

Suggested structure:
(a) Tourism provides jobs and income for local people (e.g. hotel staff, tour guides). It also increases tax revenue for the local government, which can be used to improve infrastructure.
(b) Overcrowding disturbs local people's daily lives with noise and congestion. Environmental damage such as pollution from tourist transport and littering harms the natural environment.
