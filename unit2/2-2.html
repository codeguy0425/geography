2.2 What Problems Do Tourists Bring to the Host Areas?

E7 Tourism · S1 Geography Final Revision

Economic problems

  • Rise in living costs for local people (e.g. higher land rents, property prices)
  • Over-reliance on tourism may badly affect the economy when the number of tourists decreases (e.g. during a pandemic or recession)

Environmental problems

  • Pollution (air pollution from planes and cars, water pollution from cruise ships, littering)
  • Environmental damage (habitat destruction from resort construction, coral reef damage from diving)

Social problems

  • Conflict with local people (tourist behaviour that disrespects local norms)
  • More bars and casinos may cause crime and family problems
  • Overcrowding and loud noise disturb the local people's daily life

Cultural problems

  • Changes in traditional cultures and lifestyles of local people
  • Cultural conflict between tourists and locals
  • Loss of local characteristics (shops become souvenir stalls, traditions become performances)
⚠️ MC trap: "Which is NOT an economic problem of tourism?" → "Providing jobs for local people" is a benefit, not a problem. Watch for distractors that list benefits instead of problems.
