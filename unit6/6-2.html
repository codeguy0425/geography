6.2 Making Hong Kong a Sustainable City

S1 Geography Final Revision

6.2.1 Promoting innovation and technology

The Hong Kong government applies innovation and technology across three areas — economy, environment, and society — to promote sustainable development.

GroupInitiativesHow it supports sustainable development
Economy • Support green industries — subsidies and incentives for environmental technology businesses
• Subsidise transport companies to use low-emission cars (e.g. electric buses, hybrid taxis)
• Promote green building designs in commercial buildings (e.g. BEAM Plus certification, energy-efficient lighting systems)
• Smart energy meters — help businesses track and reduce energy use
• iAM Smart platform — e-government services for businesses 		Promotes economic sustainability by shifting the economy toward green industries and cleaner transport, while reducing commercial energy consumption and operational costs
Environment • Develop waste-to-energy technology — incinerate waste to generate electricity, reducing landfill volume
• Introduce smart waste management system — sensors on bins to monitor fill levels, optimise collection routes 		Supports environmental sustainability by reducing landfill waste, generating clean energy, and making waste collection more efficient
Society • Install sensors on main roads to collect real-time traffic data — manage congestion, improve travel efficiency
• Develop mobile apps to facilitate the use of government services (e.g. iAM Smart, GovHK)
• e-Health services — online medical consultations, electronic health records
• GovWiFi — free public Wi-Fi for digital access 		Advances social sustainability by improving transport efficiency, simplifying access to government services, and enhancing digital inclusion and healthcare access for residents
📌 Exam tip: For "how can technology help sustainability" questions, organise your answer by the three groups — name a HK example in each of economy, environment, and society. "Smart lampposts" (environment + society) and "HKeMobility app" (society) are good examples to memorise.

6.2.2 Adopting people-oriented planning

The government should be people-oriented when making city development plans — focusing on the needs of citizens and addressing them in urban designs.

People's demands for medical care, education, infrastructure, recreation, and job opportunities should all be considered in urban planning.

Area of needHow urban planning addresses it
Medical careLocate hospitals and clinics within easy reach of residential areas; increase the number of healthcare professionals; plan for ageing population with more elderly healthcare facilities
EducationReserve land for schools in new town planning; build more post-secondary institutions
InfrastructureImprove transport networks (rail, bus, walking paths); reduce carbon emission through green transport and building design; improve technology and internet services for smart living
RecreationProvide more open space, parks, sports facilities, and waterfront promenades; build more cycling tracks; provide more cultural facilities (museums, performance venues, libraries)
Job opportunitiesPlan for business districts and industrial areas near residential zones to reduce commuting time; support diverse industries

By putting people's needs first, urban planning can create a more livable city for Hong Kong in the long run.

⚠️ MC trap: "People-oriented planning mainly focuses on:" → Not "maximising property profits" or "building more highways". Correct answer is "addressing citizens' needs for medical care, education, infrastructure, recreation, and jobs through urban design."
