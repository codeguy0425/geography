Key terms — quick revision cards

Sustainable development
Development that meets present needs without compromising future generations (Brundtland, 1987)
Three pillars
Social, environmental, and economic sustainability; all three must be balanced
Sustainable city
A city designed to minimise environmental impact while providing high quality of life
Smart city
A city using technology and data to improve efficiency, sustainability, and quality of life
People-oriented planning
Urban planning that focuses on citizens' needs — medical care, education, infrastructure, recreation, and jobs — to create a livable city
15-minute neighbourhood
A neighbourhood where daily needs can be met within a 15-minute walk
Universal design
Design that is accessible and usable by everyone regardless of age or ability
Smart mobility
Using technology to improve transport efficiency (e.g. real-time traffic apps)
Brundtland Report
1987 UN report "Our Common Future" that defined sustainable development
Green building
Buildings designed with energy efficiency, sustainable materials, and minimal environmental impact

Section B — True or False (1 mark each)

Q4
"Sustainable development only focuses on environmental protection."
False — it balances social, environmental, and economic needs.
Q5
"A smart city uses technology to improve efficiency and quality of life."
True — smart cities use data and technology for better urban management.

Section C — Structured Question (6 marks)

Q6

(a) Define the term "sustainable development". (2 marks)

(b) Suggest two ways Hong Kong can become a more sustainable city. (4 marks)

Suggested structure:
(a) "Development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs."
(b) Name the measure → Explain how it works → Give a HK example.
e.g. "Promoting innovation and technology across three areas — economy (e.g. subsidies for low-emission transport and green building designs), environment (e.g. waste-to-energy technology and smart waste management), and society (e.g. traffic sensors for real-time data and mobile apps for government services)."
