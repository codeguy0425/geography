6.1 Sustainable Development & Sustainable City

S1 Geography Final Revision

6.1.1 Sustainable development

Definition (Brundtland Report, 1987):

"Development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs."

Three pillars (triple bottom line)

Social
Equal access to education, healthcare, housing; social equity; community cohesion
Environmental
Protecting natural resources, reducing pollution, conserving biodiversity, using renewable energy
Economic
Long-term growth without harming environment or society; fair distribution of wealth
Social sustainability + Environmental sustainability + Economic sustainability = Sustainable Development

All three pillars must be balanced — focusing on only one or two is not true sustainable development.

👨‍🏫 Teacher's note — Common exam mistake: Students often write that sustainable development means "not harming the environment". This is incomplete — you must mention balancing ALL THREE pillars and reference future generations. The full Brundtland definition can earn 2 full marks.

6.1.2 What is a sustainable city?

A sustainable city is designed to minimise its environmental impact while providing a high quality of life for residents.

Key characteristics (13 features)

A sustainable city balances social, economic, and environmental features.

Society

FeatureDescription
Good social welfare, such as medical careProvides healthcare access, social services, and support for vulnerable groups
Includes green areas and open spaceParks, gardens, and recreational areas for public enjoyment and well-being
Provides equal education opportunitiesAccessible schooling for all, from primary to tertiary education
Provides affordable housingEnsures all income groups have access to quality housing
Encourages public participation in policymakingResidents involved in planning decisions through consultations

Economy

FeatureDescription
Supports green and high-tech industriesPromotes eco-friendly businesses and innovation-driven growth
Develops a diversified economy and creates job opportunitiesReduces reliance on any single industry; stable employment
Supports traditional and local economic activitiesPreserves local businesses, markets, and cultural industries
Provides subsidies and training for businessesHelps businesses adopt sustainable practices and upskill workers

Environment

FeatureDescription
Reduces and recycles waste at sourceMinimises waste generation; separates and recycles materials
Develops renewable energyUses solar, wind, and other clean energy sources
Improves public transport systems, such as railwayExpands rail networks to reduce car dependency and emissions
Adopts green building designs such as rooftop greeningEnergy-efficient buildings with green roofs and sustainable materials
⚠️ MC trap: "Which is NOT a characteristic of a sustainable city?" → "Building more highways for cars" — sustainable cities prioritise public transport, green spaces, and people's well-being over car-centric infrastructure.
