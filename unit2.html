Unit 2 – Is Tourism Good or Bad for Local People?

E7 Tourism · S1 Geography Final Revision
Appears in Section C
📌 Exam tip: Section C structured questions often ask you to evaluate both sides — list benefits and problems of tourism. Always give specific examples (e.g. national parks for conservation, bars/casinos for social problems). A balanced answer scores higher than a one-sided one.

2.1 What benefits do tourists bring to the host area?

Economic benefits

  • Provide jobs and incomes to local people
  • Increase tax revenue for local governments
  • Improve infrastructure and attract more investment

Social benefits

  • Develop infrastructure benefiting both tourists and locals
  • Provide more social services and financial aid
  • Raise the living standard of local people

Environmental benefits

  • Protect natural environment and wildlife (national parks, conservation)
  • Raise public awareness of environmental conservation

Cultural benefits

  • Promote cultural exchange
  • Preserve cultural heritage

2.2 What problems do tourists bring to the host areas?

Economic problems

  • Rise in living costs (higher land rents, property prices)
  • Over-reliance on tourism — economic risk when tourist numbers drop

Environmental problems

  • Pollution (air, water, litter)
  • Environmental damage (habitat destruction, coral reef damage)

Social problems

  • Conflict with local people
  • Bars and casinos → crime and family problems
  • Overcrowding and noise disturbing local people

Cultural problems

  • Changes in traditional cultures and lifestyles
  • Cultural conflict
  • Loss of local characteristics

Key terms — quick revision cards

Host area
The place or community that receives tourists
Economic benefit
Financial gain from tourism, e.g. jobs, tax revenue, investment
Social benefit
Improvements to quality of life for locals, e.g. better infrastructure and services
Environmental conservation
Protecting natural environments and wildlife, often through national parks
Cultural exchange
Interaction between tourists and local people that shares traditions and ideas
Cultural heritage
Traditions, customs, and historical sites that define a community's identity
Over-reliance on tourism
When a local economy depends too heavily on tourism, creating risk if visitor numbers fall
Overcrowding
Too many tourists in one place, causing congestion and disturbance to locals
Cultural conflict
Tension between tourists' behaviour and local customs or values
Loss of local characteristics
When tourism erodes the unique identity of a place (e.g. souvenir shops replacing traditional stores)

Exam-style practice questions

Multiple Choice (1 mark each)

Q1
Which of the following is an economic benefit of tourism?
A. Cultural exchange
B. Providing jobs to local people
C. Environmental conservation
D. Preserving cultural heritage
Q2
Which is a social problem caused by tourism?
A. Loss of tax revenue
B. Habitat destruction
C. Overcrowding
D. Cultural exchange
Q3
Over-reliance on tourism is dangerous because:
A. It increases tax revenue
B. A drop in tourist numbers hurts the economy
C. It preserves cultural heritage
D. It improves infrastructure

True or False (1 mark each)

Q4
"Tourism only brings economic benefits."
False — tourism also brings environmental, social, and cultural problems.
Q5
"Tourism can help preserve cultural heritage."
True — revenue from tourism can fund the restoration and protection of cultural sites and traditions.

Structured Question (6 marks)

Q6

Tourism brings both benefits and problems to host areas.

(a) Describe two economic benefits of tourism for local people. (2 marks)

(b) Explain two social problems caused by tourism in host areas. (4 marks)

Suggested structure:
(a) e.g. (1) Tourism provides jobs and incomes for local people (e.g. hotel staff, tour guides). (2) Tourism increases tax revenue for local governments, which can be used to improve public services.

(b) e.g. (1) Overcrowding and noise from tourists disturb local residents' daily lives. (2) Bars and casinos can lead to crime and family problems in the local community.

  • I can write a balanced answer weighing benefits against problems
